Consortium

atsec AB

atsec AB is the Swedish subsidiary of atsec information security, an independent and privately-owned company focusing on providing testing, evaluation and audit services for information security. The company operates an accredited lab for Common Criteria evaluations, and is a GSMA NESAS appointed auditor and authorised SCAS test lab for the telecom industry. In addition, atsec AB runs a private Certification Body accredited by SWEDAC (Sweden’s national accreditation body) to provide Common Criteria certifications of IT products.

Ericsson AB

Ericsson AB is a Swedish company in the Ericsson Group – one of the world's leading information and communication technology companies. Ericsson headquarters are located in Stockholm, Sweden. In Sweden, Ericsson AB employs 14500 from over 100 nationalities that work within all areas of the company - research, development, sales, production and administration. Ericsson AB is Sweden's largest employer of engineers and during the last years has hired thousands of new engineers. Ericsson AB also hires 400 student summer interns each summer and 200 students write their master’s thesis with Ericsson AB each year.

Hyker

Hyker Security is a Swedish majority owned spin-out from Saab Ventures. Hyker’s approach to cybersecurity is in protecting data rather than infrastructure over the whole lifecycle. Therefore, Hyker has engineered a platform, True Full Lifecycle Encryption (TFLE), that is enabled by our unique technology which can be built into IoT solutions, Web applications and other solutions where trust and integrity is of importance in a cloud environment. Hyker’s technical platform has attracted a lot of attention in the last twelve months due to the pandemic and digitalization in combination with securing data in transit or at rest.

RISE

The Cybersecurity Unit at RISE, the research and technology partner in CEST, is at the forefront of the cybersecurity research and development landscape in Europe. RISE Cybersecurity Unit is currently running sixteen (16) security projects, focused on IoT, cloud, software, AI/ML, and trusted/confidential computing, among others. Moreover, RISE Cybersecurity is coordinating the Swedish National Cybersecurity Research and Innovation Node (Cybernode.se) where industry, public sector, civil defense, and academia participate. RISE Cybersecurity Unit is also running the Cyber Range, one of the largest test and demonstration facility for Cybersecurity in Sweden.

This project has received funding from the Swedish Vinnova Innovation Agency with diary number No 2021-01690.
